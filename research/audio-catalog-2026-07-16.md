# Wagyu / Akaushi Audio Catalog — Grant Whitmer Rescued Phone Recordings (2017–2019)

Source: `~/audio-mining/out/` on Veron (404 Whisper transcripts). Timestamps are in seconds as embedded in the transcripts, shown as `[seconds → mm:ss]`. All quotes are lightly cleaned from Whisper output (auction chant and mis-hearings are common — see per-entry confidence notes). Dates parsed from the leading 8 digits of each filename.

**Recurring cast note:** In every auction recording below, the *pedigree commentator / breed-history narrator* is almost certainly **Grant Whitmer himself** — he repeatedly references "my good friend Cole Morris," "when I was at ABC Feeders," his own website WagyuRanch.com, and his self-description in the chef call (file 5) as "the genetic guy… reading all the pedigrees up in front of everybody at an internationally broadcast Wagyu sale." The *auctioneer* doing the bid-chant is a separate man called **Rob** (also "Lynn"/"Larry" in places). **Cole Morris** is Grant's operations partner.

### 1. The "Fred" Interview — origin story of the four U.S. foundation Wagyu bulls  ★★★ GOLD
- **File:** `iphone5s_recordings__20170422 171633-D5C9FACC.m4a.txt`
- **Date:** 2017-04-22 · **Duration:** ~25 min (1487 s)
- **TYPE:** In-person interview (recorded on the floor of a Wagyu auction, side conversation)
- **PEOPLE:** Grant Whitmer (interviewer; gives his own number "254-583-2436" and name at the end) and **"Fred"** — an original importer/owner of the first Wagyu bulls brought to the United States. Also named: Morris Whitney (the man sent to Japan), Dennis Wynn (the vet), Shimbayashi (Japanese university chancellor), Dr. Cross (Texas A&M), Ralph Valdez (whose herd Grant later bought out).
- **SUMMARY:** This is the crown jewel of the whole archive: Grant corners an elderly original importer and records the firsthand history of how the first four Wagyu bulls (Mount Fuji, Mazda, and two others rendered by Whisper as "Rishon/Rueshaw" and "Chuto/Judo") reached America. Fred recounts Morris Whitney being sent to Japan to buy the bulls, the animals being quarantined on an island in Japan, routed through Canada into Montana, then to Colorado State and finally Texas (San Antonio → Georgetown). He details the three original corporations (KBPI = Kobe Beef Producers Inc.; KCCI = Kobe Cattle Company Inc.; and Wagyu Breeders Inc.), the vet Dennis Wynn who ran up huge bills and "fleeced" everyone, the bankruptcy-court battle, and how Fred ultimately won the four bulls by tendering a certified check to the court. He then describes blood-typing the bulls at College Station, which proved Wagyu are a **distinct genetic breed** — not Bos taurus, not Bos indicus. He owned the bulls until they died, held a complete dispersal sale in 1993 (13,000–14,000 straws of semen), and created an entity called "Semen Express." Grant, audibly thrilled, says he's been wanting to meet Fred "for so long" and promises hours of follow-up calls.
- **PEARLS:**
  - `[86.7 → 1:27]` "They sent him [Morris Whitney] over there, and he found these bulls… he was able to purchase them because Japan recognized that [Wagyu] is a national emblem in Japan."
  - `[146.5 → 2:27]` "They both were put on an island in quarantine. The Japanese government allowed that… nothing could be imported from Japan [to the U.S.]. That's a little bit of politics." (Confirms the Canada→Montana routing, and that they did **not** come through Mexico — "everybody said the cattle came in through Mexico… that's a great story.")
  - `[709.7 → 11:49]` "I give the certified check, and the court gave me the four bulls and the corporations… he finally got screwed himself." (How Fred won the foundation bulls out of bankruptcy court.)
  - `[1067.9 → 17:48]` The blood-typing revelation: "He concluded, looking at all the chromosomal link-ups, he said these bulls aren't Bos taurus, they're not Bos indicus… They're a genetic breed all by themselves. **Wagyu.**"
  - `[1103.3 → 18:23]` "Wagyu in Japanese means Japanese cattle — *wa* is Japan or Japanese, and *yu* is cattle."
  - `[1156.8 → 19:17]` "In 1993 I had a complete dispersal sale… I had about 13,000, 14,000 straws of semen on these four bulls." (Then created "Semen Express" to distribute it at ~$15/straw.)
  - `[865.7 → 14:26]` The document proving "[the youngest bull] as a two-year-old was the national grand champion for two-year-olds in Japan."
- **Confidence:** Medium-high on substance, but Whisper garbles the bull names badly (Mount Fuji, Mazda are clear; the other two swim between "Rishon/Rueshaw/Chuto/Judo"). Line names are transcribed as "Juho/Totori/Tajima" (Mazda = Totori line; Mt. Fuji = Tajima line). Treat individual proper nouns as needing verification, but the narrative arc is coherent and consistent.

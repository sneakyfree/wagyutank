# DRAFT — Outreach to the American Wagyu Association (data partnership)

**Status:** DRAFT for Grant's review. Do not send until reviewed/edited.
**Send to:** American Wagyu Association — general/executive contact (office@wagyu.org,
per their Digital Beef placeholder page) and/or the Executive Director. The
association owns the data policy; Helical is only the platform vendor, so the AWA is
the right first contact. Consider a short phone call first (+1 208-262-8100) and
this note as the written follow-up.
**From:** Grant Whitmer, Founder, WagyuTank.com · office@wagyutank.com · Salt Lake City, Utah

---

**Subject: A respectful request — public-data access to celebrate AWA-registered sires & dams**

Dear American Wagyu Association,

I'm Grant Whitmer, the founder of WagyuTank.com, a knowledge hub and marketplace
built to serve Wagyu breeders. First, a simple thank-you: the AWA's decades of work
— especially holding the DNA record on the original foundation imports — is the
reason a verifiable Wagyu pedigree exists in this country at all. We don't take that
lightly, and we'd like to build with you rather than around you.

We're developing a feature we call **Breed Impactors** — a way to recognize the
sires and dams that have shaped the breed, ranked honestly by registered progeny,
with digital certificates a breeder can be proud to hang in the barn. Our aim is
celebration and education: driving attention and traffic *toward* the breed, toward
registered animals, and toward AWA members.

To do this accurately and respectfully, I'm writing to ask a straightforward
question: **does the AWA (through Helical or otherwise) offer a public-data API or a
sanctioned data-sharing arrangement** we could use to display registered animals,
pedigrees, and progeny counts — always credited to the AWA, always linking back to
the official record?

We noticed the registry recently moved to Helical, and we want to be clear about our
intent: we will not scrape or circumvent your platform. We'd rather have a legitimate,
blessed feed, on whatever terms you set — read-only, rate-limited, attributed, and
structured however you prefer. If a formal API isn't available, we'd welcome a
conversation about what a partnership could look like, including how it might benefit
your members (recognition certificates that promote breeders, links back to the
registry, and renewed public interest in registration itself).

If this is worth exploring, I'd be grateful for a short call at your convenience.
Thank you for your time, and for everything the association has done for the breed.

With respect and gratitude,
Grant Whitmer
Founder, WagyuTank.com
office@wagyutank.com
